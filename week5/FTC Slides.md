# The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
### A Graduate Introduction

---

## Part 1: The Connection

If $f$ is continuous on $[a, b]$ and $F(x) = \int_a^x f(t)\,dt$, then

$$F'(x) = f(x)$$

This means **integration and differentiation are inverse operations**.

---

## Summary

- FTC Part 1: the derivative of an integral recovers the integrand
- FTC Part 2: $\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b) - F(a)$
- These results hold for any continuous $f$

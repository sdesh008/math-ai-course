Welcome to the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus lecture.

The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus connects differentiation and integration.

Part one: If F of x equals the integral from a to x of f of t dt, then the derivative of F equals f of x.
This means the derivative of an accumulation function recovers the original function.

Part two: A definite integral from a to b of f of x dx equals F of b minus F of a, where F is any antiderivative of f.
This allows us to evaluate definite integrals by using antiderivatives.

For example, integrate x squared from one to three.
An antiderivative is x cubed over three.
Evaluating from one to three gives twenty six over three.

The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus makes calculus practical by turning area computation into a subtraction of antiderivatives.
It also shows that integration and differentiation are inverse operations.
